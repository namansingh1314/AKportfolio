About

Aakriti Kushwaha is a passionate PhD student in Photochemical Sciences at Bowling Green State University, where she is deeply involved in cutting-edge research on light-responsive polymeric materials. With a strong foundation in chemistry, Aakriti has developed a specialized focus on synthesizing biomass-derived photoinitiators and exploring novel excited state photo transformations as part of her doctoral thesis.

  • Subject Knowledge: Gel Permeation Chromatography (GPC), High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC), Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GCMS), Matrix-assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization (MALDI), Cyclic voltammetry, IR spectroscopy, X-Ray Diffraction (XRD), Titrimetric analysis, Laser flash photolysis
  • Tools & Instruments: Photoluminescence Spectrometer (FLS-1000), NMR Spectroscopy Machine, pH Meter, Potentiometer, FTIR, XRD, GPC, HPLC, GCMS
  • Maintenance: Regular inspection and calibration of Ecosec-GPC instrument, HPLC Waters, XRD-Bruker D8 Quest
  • Programming Languages: C++, Q-Basic, Python, SQL (primary level)
